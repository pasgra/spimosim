About the model

The Bornholdt model was proposed by Stefan Bornholdt in 2001 [1] as an Ising model with stock market like behavior with bubbles, crashes between power law distributed phases of stability.

In the original paper Bornholdt used an 32x32 lattice with periodic boundary condition and random serial updates.

The parameters are the inverse temperature β, the global coupling α and the local coupling j. 

[1] S. Bornholdt, Expectation bubbles in a spin model of markets: Intermittency from
frustration across scales, Int. J. Mod. Phys. C, Vol. 12, No. 5 (2001) 667-674.
