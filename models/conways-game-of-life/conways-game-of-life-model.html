About the model

Conway's Game of Life[1] is a mathematical game with very simple rules but complex dynamics.

Rules

Every cell (pixel) has eight neighbors. A cell with less than 2 neighbors dies from loneliness and a cell with more than 3 neighbors dies from overpopulation. If a dead cell has 3 neighbors, a new one is born. 

[1] Martin Gardner. "Mathematical Games: The fantastic combinations of John Conway’s
new solitaire game "life"". In: Scientific American 223 (1970), S. 120–123.
